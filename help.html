Welcome

Welcome to the help page for Join, your guide to using our kanban project management tool. Here you'll find an overview of what Join is, how it can benefit you, and a step-by-step guide on how to use it.

What is Join?

Join is a kanban-based project management tool designed and built by a group of dedicated students as part of their web development bootcamp curriculum. Kanban, a Japanese term meaning "billboard", is a highly effective method to visualize work, limit work-in-progress, and maximize efficiency (or flow). Join leverages the principles of kanban to help users manage their tasks and projects in an intuitive, visual interface.

It is important to note that Join is designed as an educational exercise and is not intended for extensive business usage. While we strive to ensure the best possible user experience, please be aware that Join may not offer the same level of functionality and reliability as commercial project management tools.

How to use it

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use Join:

1. Exploring the Board

When you log in to Join, you'll find a default board. This board represents your project and contains four default lists: "To Do", "In Progress", "Await feedback" and "Done". Each list contains cards that represent individual tasks or items.

2. Creating Contacts

In Join, you can add contacts to collaborate on your projects. Go to the "Contacts" section, click on "Add new contact", and fill in the required information. Once added, these contacts can be assigned to tasks and they will be notified of any updates.

3. Adding Cards

Now that you've explored the board and added your contacts, you can start adding cards. Cards represent individual tasks. Click the "+" button under the appropriate list to add a new card. Fill in the task details like task name, description, due date, assignees, etc.

4. Moving Cards

As the task moves from one stage to another, you can reflect that on the board by dragging and dropping the card from one list to another. This visual representation of task progression makes it easy to keep track of project status.

Enjoy using Join!
